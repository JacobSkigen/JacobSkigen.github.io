I am a newly minted PhD from the biomedical engineering department of the University of Delaware, currently looking for a post-doctoral position to apply and expand my skills in neuromechanics through collaborative work on impactful projects. During my time at UD, I got to work with Dr. Elisa Arch in the Orthotics & Prosthetics for Enhanced Mobility Lab where my research focused on how the use of a passive-dynamic ankle-foot orthosis (PD-AFO) affects the biomechanics, energetics, and muscle activity of the post-stroke lower limb system. Before Delaware, I got my start in gait rehabilitation research at North Carolina State University working under Dr. Helen (He) Huang in the Neuromuscular Rehabilitation Engineering Laboratory with a focus on gait and postural control in lower limb amputees.

Intentend to pursue a career in academia based around teaching and research. I'm excited to establish my own line of research and make meaningful contributions to the field.

I am always open to a good science conversation, so if you have insights, questions, or advice please feel free to contact me.
